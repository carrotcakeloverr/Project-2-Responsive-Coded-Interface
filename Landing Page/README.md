# Project Title
Cafe Saiko Landing Page 

## Project Description
I made a landing product page for one of Orlando's most popular local matcha/cafe place: Saiko. Highlighting their 
newest matcha flavor Mango Stticky Matcha 

## Intended User or Player
This interface is designed for mostly people that like going to coffee shops, or reside in those spaces to do homework,
hangout or conversate, mainly college students and young adults. 

## User / Player Goal
The player should be able to scroll though the page, understand the product that is advertised, being able to buy it easyly and also look at the other options that the shop has. 

## Inspiration Interfaces

Matcha Protein landing page
https://more-nutrition.webflow.io/

I like how you can see a clear picture of the product, as well as a button that says “buy now” so the viewer has easy access. I also noticed the tiny boxes on the right that are a call to action to also market other products within the store/business, it creates a hierarchy between the main product and opportunity to browse throughout the website. I was also interested in the typography being a bold San Serif that is easy to read and falls within the color scheme. I want to adapt the simplicity of the color scheme with the bold typography on my own interface, as well as the simple layout so it is readable and easy to browse.

Savor restaurant landing page.
https://bestawards.co.nz/digital/small-scale-websites/resn/savor/

I noticed how their layout is highly visual, instead of having a lot of different texts, the landing page has simplicity but clearness with using a single image to wrap around the whole screen, making the product stand out and creating a sense of excitement or interest in the user. The logo and menu bar are also pushed to the top of the web page, with the logo being bigger than the menu to emphasize it, making the user prone to having an easier time remembering the branding. I also noticed the simplicity of the list that is on the second page that guides the user’s view through ingredients and the making of the product. I want to create a menu bar that has the same simplicity and overall aesthetic with the logo of the restaurant to be very noticeable.

Alfredo restaurant landing page.
https://alfredogelado.pt


For this interface I really enjoy the list that is seen on the first page with bold typography, easy to navigate because of the different hierarchy decisions that are evident within the body copy and main titles of the products. The products on the second page are also striking because of the clarity and minimalism of the page, users can visualize the product clearly and have a visual idea of what it’s going to look like. With simple typography on the upper side, it is easy to navigate and select each item. I want to bring the simplicity of putting suggested items on my interface with clear pictures just like the second image to emphasize marketing and make it accessible for the user.
